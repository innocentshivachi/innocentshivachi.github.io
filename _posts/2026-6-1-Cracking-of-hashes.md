In cybersecurity, password security remains one of the most critical areas of defense. Tools like John the Ripper and Hydra are widely used in ethical hacking and penetration testing to test the strength of authentication systems and identify weak credentials.

## John the ripper

john  is a powerful offline password cracking tool used to recover weak passwords from hashed files. It helps security professionals evaluate how strong stored password hashes are and whether they can be easily cracked using dictionary or brute-force techniques.That's  why it  is  the mostly used.
![John the ripper image](/assets/john.jpeg) {: width="600"}

## Hydra

is a fast and flexible online brute-force attack tool used to test login systems such as SSH, FTP, HTTP, and more. It allows security testers to simulate real-world login attacks and assess how well systems resist repeated authentication attempts.
![Hydra image](/assets/hydra.jpeg) {: width="600"}

## Crack the Station

Hands-on labs like Crack the Station (commonly found in platforms such as TryHackMe) bring these concepts together in a practical environment. They help learners understand how attackers exploit weak credentials and how defenders can strengthen authentication systems through better password policies and security configurations.
